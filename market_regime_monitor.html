What this dashboard tracks

Three forward-looking indicators that historically deteriorate before equity drawdowns. The composite score weights each equally and translates the regime into a single 0–100 reading.

  • Oil price — sustained levels above ~$90/bbl tend to reignite inflation, restrict Fed easing, and pressure margins.
  • Capex (NEWORDER) — core capital goods orders are a leading indicator of business investment; the AI capex cycle shows up here.
  • Unemployment — the Sahm Rule (+0.5pp above 12-month low on the 3-month average) has reliably preceded recessions.

How to read the signals

Each indicator gets a score from 0 (bearish) to 100 (bullish) based on its level and trend versus historical thresholds. The composite is the equal-weighted average.

  • 70–100 · Bull regime — fundamentals support continued upside; framework intact.
  • 40–70 · Mixed — at least one indicator deteriorating; tighten risk discipline.
  • 0–40 · Bear regime — multiple signals broken; defensive positioning warranted.

This is a regime filter, not a market timing tool. Use it to inform position sizing and risk posture, not to make all-in/all-out calls.
